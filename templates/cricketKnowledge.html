Cricket Knowledge

Basic Cricket Knowledge

  • Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players each on a field with a 22-yard pitch at the center.
  • The game involves three formats: Test cricket (5 days), One Day Internationals (50 overs), and T20 (20 overs per team).
  • Each team bats once and bowls once in limited overs formats (ODI & T20).
  • The batting team’s objective is to score more runs than the bowling team.
  • Each over consists of six balls bowled by a single bowler.
  • The objective of the bowler is to dismiss the batsman by getting them out in various ways (e.g., bowled, caught, LBW).
  • The batsman aims to defend the stumps and score runs through various shots, such as driving, pulling, and cutting.
  • The bowler’s role is to bowl the ball, aiming to get the batsman out while preventing runs.
  • The fielding team is spread across the ground to catch the ball and stop the batsmen from scoring runs.
  • A run is scored when a batsman hits the ball and runs to the opposite end of the pitch, switching places with the other batsman.

Batting Knowledge and Rules

  • Each batsman must wear protective gear such as a helmet, gloves, and pads to avoid injury.
  • The batsman’s role is to defend the stumps and hit the ball to score runs.
  • The most common ways to get out as a batsman are being bowled, caught, or dismissed LBW (leg before wicket).
  • The batsman can score runs by hitting the ball to the boundary or by running between the wickets.
  • A boundary is four runs if the ball touches the ground before going over the boundary line, and six runs if it crosses without touching the ground.
  • If the batsman is bowled, the wicketkeeper must remove the bails for the dismissal to be complete.
  • When facing a bowler, the batsman can decide to leave the ball if it’s outside the off-stump or play a shot to defend the ball.
  • The batting team rotates the strike when batsmen change ends after running one or more runs.
  • The umpire signals a wide ball if the ball is delivered outside the batsman’s reach and not a legitimate delivery.
  • A no-ball is declared if the bowler oversteps the crease or delivers an illegal delivery such as a high bouncer.

Bowling Knowledge and Rules

  • The bowler is allowed to bowl a maximum of six balls in an over.
  • In limited-overs formats, bowlers are restricted to a specific number of overs per game (e.g., 10 overs in ODI, 4 overs in T20).
  • The bowler must release the ball with their arm straight (no "throwing").
  • Different types of deliveries include fast balls, spin balls, bouncers, yorkers, and off-breaks.
  • A fast bowler typically aims for pace and accuracy to challenge the batsman’s defenses.
  • A spin bowler uses slower deliveries and twists the ball to deceive the batsman.
  • The bowler may bowl with different lengths: short-pitched (bouncers), full-pitched (yorkers), or good-length deliveries.
  • When a bowler delivers a wide ball or no-ball, the batting side gets an extra delivery and one run is added to the score.
  • Some bowlers specialize in taking wickets by deceiving the batsman with variations in speed and flight.
  • A bowler can dismiss a batsman in several ways, including bowled, caught, LBW, or stumped (with the wicketkeeper’s help).

Interesting Cricket Facts

  • The longest Test match in history was played between South Africa and England in 1939, lasting for 12 days.
  • The fastest century in cricket was scored by Shahid Afridi in just 37 balls in 1996.
  • The first-ever international cricket match was played between the United States and Canada in 1844.
  • The highest individual score in a Test match is 400*, achieved by Brian Lara in 2004.
  • The most runs in a single ODI match was scored by Virat Kohli, who hit 183 runs against Sri Lanka in 2012.
  • The highest ever team score in a T20 match is 263/3, achieved by Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL 2013.
  • The shortest ODI match lasted just 18 overs per side due to rain (West Indies vs. Australia, 1999).
  • The longest cricket match was a 199-day Test match between Australia and South Africa in 1939.
  • The first-ever cricket World Cup was held in 1975 in England, and it was won by the West Indies.
  • The first player to score a century in a T20 International was Chris Gayle from the West Indies in 2007.
